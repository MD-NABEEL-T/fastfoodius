1. The main payment gateway fee

For Razorpay standard pricing in India:

Typical rates (approx):

UPI: up to ~2%

Credit / Debit cards: up to ~2%

Net banking / wallets: up to ~2%

So your assumption of around 2% is basically correct.

But there are two details many people miss:

GST on the fee

Razorpay charges 18% GST on their fee.

Example:

Student pays ₹100
Gateway fee (2%) = ₹2
GST on fee (18%) ≈ ₹0.36

Total deduction ≈ ₹2.36

Canteen receives ≈ ₹97.64

So effectively it’s about 2.36%, not exactly 2%.

2. No monthly subscription

Good news:

No setup fee

No monthly fee

No annual fee

You only pay per transaction.

3. Settlement timing

Money doesn’t go instantly to the bank.

Typical settlement:

T+2 working days

Example:
Payment on Monday → bank by Wednesday/Thursday.

4. Refund costs

If you refund a customer:

Razorpay usually does NOT return the gateway fee.

Example:

Customer paid ₹100
You refund ₹100

You still lose the ₹2–₹2.36 fee.

For a canteen this usually isn’t a big issue.

5. Chargebacks / disputes (rare but real)

If a card user disputes a payment:

Banks may charge ₹300–₹1000 dispute fee.

But in a college canteen with UPI, this almost never happens.

6. Hidden charges?

There aren’t really “hidden” ones, but some optional things exist:

Possible extra costs if you use advanced features:

International cards → ~3%

Instant settlements → small fee

Smart routing / advanced tools → optional

Your canteen app probably won’t use these.

7. Your platform fee idea

Your thinking is actually common.

Example in your app:

Food price = ₹100
Platform fee (2%) = ₹2

Customer pays ₹102.

Razorpay fee (≈2.36% of ₹102) ≈ ₹2.40

Canteen receives about ₹99.60.

So the canteen almost gets the full ₹100.

This makes it easier to convince them:

They don’t lose margin

Students pay small convenience fee

They get faster orders and less queue.

8. One rule you should follow

Be transparent in the UI.

Instead of hiding it in food price, show something like:

Item total: ₹100

Convenience fee: ₹2

Total: ₹102

Students usually accept this.
